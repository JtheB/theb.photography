Enlarging Black and White films

Or why I love my bath without windows

My dad came to visit me and brought me some of his old papers and a Jobo enlarger. Since I have a bathroom with no windows and plenty of space on my washing maschine, I thought of using it as a darkroom to develop some enlarged photos on film.
Before we started, some issues occured. First the redlight lamp was so old, the colourgiving foil on it crumpeled and we had to adapt and glue some stripes of tape on the rips. It may look weird and it gave the dark room a funky disco look. We had no problems with light developing the film unwanted. From my collection of developed 35mm films, I chose my favorite ones. Most of the paper was older than 20 years, so we hoped the papers to be not to washed out, As you can see below, they were rather harsh in their blacks.
The nex issue we had, was that the older developer had oxidized. But thoughtfully my dad bought some shortly before traveling to me. So we prepared the bathroom and all the baths we needed and started!
We used an aperture of 4.5 for most of the photos and the time differed between 14-21s. On some (Mostly the Ṕrague photos with sky partly covering the photo) we tried to use some tricks to not overdevelop the light parts by "Abwedeln" as it is called here in Germany.

One can see, that in some cases the contrast is quite flat. These images are the original ID-11 films. 1+1 created the best results with the APX-100. Sadly the PANF-50 was overdeveloped. Maybe next time it will work properly!

All images are in their original form with just a few Lightroom tweakings.
